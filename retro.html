Organize the Life You Share in One Place

Get the template for a 30-minute, 4-question relationship check-in, so you and your can partner show up for each other and be on the same page.

What you get

A Place to Share Notes and To-Dos

1. Shareable spreadsheet

You'll get a Google Sheet to guide and keep track of your relationship check-ins. It's easy to share with your partner, so both of you can refer back to it during the week.

2. Four useful questions

Inspired by retrospectives in software engineering, your Google Sheet includes four questions to help you sync up on chores, fun things to try, what's working, and what's not.

3. Action items

As things come up—like a bill that needs paying—you can assign action items to you or your partner. That way, both of you know what's happening, and who's taking care of it.

How it works

30 Minutes. 4 Questions. Once a Week.

Pick a time

Schedule 30 minutes of face-to-face time. (Sunday evening after dinner is a popular choice.)

Open your template

Start your check-in by opening up the Google Sheet. Inside, you'll find 4 discussion questions.

Discuss

Set a timer for 30 minutes. Then, discuss each question, syncing up on chores, date ideas, and goals.

Reconnect

After your check-in, thank each other. Celebrate with snuggles, ice cream, or whatever you enjoy best!

Why check-ins?

Get on the Same Page

Save conversations for later

Knowing you have a relationship check-in coming up, you can comfortably put a pin in discussions that might otherwise throw off your day.

Lighten your mental load

When you get on the same page about chores and problems that need solving, no one person has to be responsible for remembering all the things.

Show up for each other

In the craziness of life, check-ins help you make time to support the person you care about most, and to make sure your relationship is always improving.
